MORPHING x JOHANN STRAUSS
The 200th anniversary in 2025

Based in Vienna, the heart of the former Danube Monarchy, the cosmopolitan VIENNA MORPHING ORCHESTRA is more than familiar with the works of the Strauss dynasty. The 200th anniversary of Johann Strauss's son is the occasion for a wonderful musical celebration! From gallops to polkas to the most famous waltzes, nothing is missing. Brahms was already enchanted by Strauss: "He is the only one I envy - he is dripping with music, he always comes up with something." Following the brilliant success of the collaboration with singer Timna Brauer in November 2023 at the Vienna Konzerthaus she will now also appear as a guest soloist at the Strauss Gala. Since its foundation, the MORPHING ORCHESTRA has been characterized by collaborations with musicians far removed from classical music. And Johann actually swings too! Timna Brauer swings unconventionally and humorously through the sound palace of the Waltz King. She juggles virtuosically between classical music, jazz and fantasy, Strauss and Mozart in a different way.

MYSTICAL MORPHING
with Andreas Scholl
Bach / Pärt

The internationally celebrated countertenor Andreas Scholl and the sought-after Viennese ensemble Morphing Chamber Orchestra next autumn will present a program for our time:
the concert mystical morphing on October 4th, 2023 brings together the baroque master J.S.Bach with the contemporary icon Arvo Pärt and immerses the audience in the music of silence. A moment of calm in challenging times. The current album Stabat Mater, which contains the works by Arvo Pärt played in the program in this line-up, was nominated in four categories for the ECHO Klassik Prize and the Edison Classic Award. The MCO, soloist Andreas Scholl, the recording itself and artistic director Tomasz Wabnic in his role as primusinter pares, as a conductor from the ensemble, were honored with nominations.
An evening with a world-class singer: appearances at the Salzburg Festival, at the New York MET and at the London Festival The classical spectacle Last Night of the Proms gave Andreas Scholl a unique selling point as a countertenor. Its remarkable Voice is documented on a variety of recordings - Antonio Vivaldi's Cum Dederit, which appeared in the James Bond film, received particular attention Specter sounds. Andreas Scholl's association with Austria includes, among other things, a professorship for baroque singing at the Salzburg Mozarteum. Mystical Morphing celebrates the baroque master Johann Sebastian Bach. The German composer is known for both his virtuosity He is known for his keyboard works and harmonious works of art as well as for his deeply felt spirituality. His music harmonizes and contrasts equally in this program with that of the contemporary master Pärt, who shares the pursuit of spirituality with him.
In addition to selected Arias and cantatas by J.S.Bach (“Oh stay, my dearest life”, “Out of deep distress I cry out to you”, slumber your dull eyes”), Stylistically and emotionally performed by countertenor Andreas Scholl, there is also a new arrangement of the Organ Sonata No. 4 by Tomasz Wabnic discover - and last but not least, enjoy the majestic air. The MCO presents Bach in chamber music harmony, sometimes in smaller, sometimes larger ensembles.

Featured Artists
  • Arvo Pärt, composer
  • Tomasz Wabnic, artistic director & viola
  • Andreas Scholl, singer
MORPHING FOR HUMANITY FOR THE MIDDLE EAST

Music is the only universal language that is able to overcome opposites! “For us musicians, it is not only a privilege, but also a responsibility not to tire of spreading the message of peace, solidarity and building bridges,” says the artistic director of the Morphing Chamber Orchestra, Tomasz Wabnic. With the charity concert for more humanity, the orchestra would like to express its solidarity with the victims of terror and war in Israel and Palestine. Morphing for Humanity, change towards more humanity, is both a call and a program title. At Timna Brauer's request, the line-up of soloists was supplemented by the Palestinian musician Marwan Abado. After costs have been covered, all proceeds go to the Red Cross for emergency aid on site. We look forward to your valued visit!

Opening Concert at World Economic Forum, Davos

The Annual Meeting's opening concert, entitled "I Sea You", is a powerful cultural statement of unity and collaboration, and a call to action to world leaders and policy-makers to implement the appropriate and required agreements to protect and preserve the coral reefs of the Northern Red Sea. Performed by the Morphing Chamber Orchestra, featuring world-renowned oud virtuoso Joseph Tawadros and percussionist James Tawadros, "I Sea You" also features exclusive performances by internationally acclaimed award- winning artists including soprano Fatma Said, pianist, composer and arranger Tim Allhoff, virtuoso Farah Siraj, guitarist, composer and producer Andreas Arnold and singer, songwriter Achinoam Nini (Noa), who is joined by her longstanding musical collaborator Gil Dor. The concert includes a specially created immersive backdrop featuring footage of the Red Sea corals, filmed by environmentalist and deep-sea diver Nouf Alosaimi, as well as imagery provided by Coral Morphologic.

Tomasz Wabnic

Tomasz Wabnic is the founder and artistic director of the Morphing Chamber Orchestra and violist of the Morphing String Quartet in Vienna and of the Huberman String Trio. Leading peer musicians and audiences around the world are amazed by the bold energy and inventive drive of this multi-faceted artist. The 2020/21 season sees Tomasz Wabnic invited to perform in more than a hundred of the world's leading venues, from New York's Carnegie Hall and Teatro alla Scala to the Golden Hall of the Vienna Musikverein.

During the summer of 2020 he performed, together with Roberto Alagna and Alexandra Kurzak, in the Metropolitan Opera Stars Live in Concert series. Recent appearances by Tomasz Wabnic also include performances at the Amsterdam Concertgebouw, Moscow's Philharmonic Hall, the Tchaikovsky Hall of the Moscow Conservatory, the legendary Teatro Amazonas in Manaus, Brazil, and the amazing Tokyo Opera City Concert Hall. Founded by Tomas Wabnic in Vienna in 2006, the Morphing Chamber Orchestra has quickly established itself as one of the most sought-after soloist ensembles at international level. The orchestra, composed of fixed members all of whom are award-winning soloists from fifteen countries, has its resident series at the famous Eroica Hall of Vienna's Lobkowitz Palace has recorded widely for SONY Classical, BMG Classics, and Universal Music. Following his concept of a congenial combination of musical styles and art forms in an unusually intense rehearsal process, Tomasz Wabnic established close musical partnerships with other extraordinary artists as diverse as star soprano Alexandra Kurzak, US vocal artist Bobby McFerrin, counter-tenor legend Andreas Scholl, Canadian jazz singer Sarah Reid, the Egyptian-Australian oud virtuoso Joseph Tawadros and major composers such as Arvo Pärt and Krzysztof Penderecki.

Tomasz Wabnic is also the founder of Capella Czestochoviensis and served as its artistic director from 2006 to 2012. Now the artistic director of Capella Claromontana since 2012, he leads another world-class, Early Music ensemble dedicated at unearthing lost music especially of the Polish Baroque era. Mr. Wabnic produced world- premiere recordings of several works by Daubeck and Gotschalk. The previously lost gems rediscovered by Tomasz Wabnic at the music archives of the Częstochowa monastery of Mons Clara include several works by W.A. Mozart and Joseph Haydn. In 2013 Tomasz Wabnic, together with brothers Sándor and Ádám Jávorkai, founded the Huberman String Trio. The Trio presented its critically acclaimed recording of J.S. Bach's Goldberg Variations (arr. Dmitri Sitkovetsky) in 2016. Recent appearances of Mr. Wabnic with the Huberman Trio included performances at the Rheingau Musik Festival and at NOSPR Hall in Katowice. Born in Wrocław, Poland, into a family of musicians, Tomasz Wabnic received his education as a violinist and a violist at the conservatories and universities of Bydgoszcz and Poznan in the violin class of the internationally famed violinist Jadwiga Kaliszewska as well as at Vienna's Musik und Kunst Privatuniversität as a member of the highly regarded viola class of Herwig Zelle and of the chamber music masterclass of the Altenberg Trio Wien. He graduated with honours. Numerous awards and honours were bestowed on Tomasz Wabnic as a soloist and chamber musician. With his Fidelio Quartet, he was a finalist in the 2001 International Chamber Music Competition in Melbourne. Morphing: the combination of several works of music and other art forms through seamless tranisition, is a concept which fascinated Tomasz Wabnic since his infant days: In his parents' living room, he used to play two or three records and the radio all at the same time, with the idea of creating a new sound. Today he lives with his family in Vienna.
